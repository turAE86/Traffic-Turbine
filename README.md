## 📌 Overview

The **Traffic Turbine Project** is an innovative and sustainable engineering concept that focuses on generating electricity from the kinetic energy of moving vehicles. As urban areas continue to grow and traffic density increases, this project aims to transform a common urban challenge into a renewable energy opportunity.

By installing turbine-based mechanisms beneath or alongside roads, the system captures mechanical energy generated by vehicle motion and converts it into electrical energy. This energy can then be used for street lighting, traffic signals, or stored for future use.

---

## 🌍 Problem Statement

* Increasing energy demand in urban areas
* Heavy reliance on non-renewable energy sources
* Wastage of kinetic energy from continuous traffic flow
* Need for smart and sustainable city solutions

---

## 💡 Proposed Solution

The project proposes a **traffic-driven turbine system** that:

* Captures kinetic energy from vehicles passing over or near the system
* Converts mechanical motion into electrical energy using generators
* Stores or directly utilizes generated power for local infrastructure

---

## ⚙️ Working Principle

1. 🚗 Vehicles pass over a specially designed turbine mechanism
2. 🔄 The force exerted causes the turbine blades or rollers to rotate
3. ⚡ The rotational motion drives a generator
4. 🔋 Electrical energy is produced and stored in batteries or used instantly
